Production rules

Format locks

Send from
Ann's name (not brand)
Format
Plain text feel. Optional small product image in email 4 + 5.
Merge tag
{{ first_name }} at start of every email. Fallback "there" if blank.
Discount
25% off sitewide (note: original EOFY pack mocked at 30% — confirm 25% is final)
Window
~7 days live. Email 5 fires 48h before close.
Audience
Full Klaviyo list (subscribers + customers + warm). Email 1 + 5 sometimes excludes recent purchasers.
Suppression
Suppress people who bought via product-launch email exclusive in last 14 days from email 1+2 (don't punish loyal buyers).
Reply tracking
Email 1+2 are short — engagement-only sends. Email 4+5 = conversion.

Locks needed before send

  • Confirm discount: 25% off (this dashboard) vs 30% (existing EOFY pack)
  • Lock sale start date + end date (sets all [DAY DATE TBC] placeholders)
  • Confirm 7-day live window (or shorter/longer)
  • Shop link finalised (used in email 4 + 5)
  • Confirm new products included in sale (currently yes)
  • Klaviyo flow built + segment built (suppress launch-buyers from emails 1+2)
  • Coordinate with existing EOFY image-ad pack (cream/sage lockups) for visual consistency if any creative goes in body

Why this sequence works

  • → Email 1 "Don't buy yet" = pattern interrupt. Engagement signal to Klaviyo. Different from every other brand inbox.
  • → Twice-a-year framing in email 2 = real scarcity, not manufactured.
  • → 48hr countdown email 3 = builds anticipation, sets exact start date.
  • → "5 months till next sale" anchor in email 4+5 = concrete cost of waiting.
  • → "Your sign" framing in email 3+4 = Type C voice without leaning identity-heavy.
  • → New products mentioned every email after #2 = doubles awareness for cold/warm who missed launch sequence.

Burned / don't do

  • "Ends anytime" — keep window vague but never use this phrase (Ann scrub)
  • "Don't miss out" / "Hurry" / countdown timer images (too aggressive)
  • Em dashes (Ann scrub rule, commas/periods only)
  • Big banner sale image at top of email (kills plain-text feel)
  • "Once in a lifetime" / "limited stock" if not literally true
  • Multiple CTA buttons per email (1 inline link max in 4+5)
  • Discount in subject line of emails 1+2 (saves reveal for email 3)
  • "Buy now" hard-sell language
  • Type C / Certified Chaos identity copy in subject lines (per EOFY pack rule, sale lane stays DR not identity)
  • Repeating exact same body copy across emails 4+5 (rotate angles even though messaging similar)

Voice variance across the 5 emails

All 5 emails sit in similar territory (sale is coming → sale is here → sale ends). To stop them feeling repetitive, rotate the framing angle.

Email 1
Pattern interrupt. "Don't buy from us." Stops the scroll because no other brand says this.
Email 2
Scarcity primer. "Only do this twice a year." Sets the anchor before discount is revealed.
Email 3
Specifics + urgency. 48hr countdown. 25% revealed. "Next isn't till November." Cost-of-waiting framing.
Email 4
Invitation. "Your sign." Permission-to-buy tone. Links live.
Email 5
Last-call concrete. Time-bound + scarcity bookend. "5 months till next time."
